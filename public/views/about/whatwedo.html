What We Do

School Arts Fund is a nonprofit organization founded to fund Performing and Visual Arts programs in Houston and surrounding areas public schools.

The School Arts Fund has found a severe lack of funding of the Performing, Visual, and Industrial Arts in the schools. In some schools, there simply are no programs. In the schools where there are Arts programs, they are severely underfunded. Students and Teachers are often working without the essential supplies they need. Art classes without paper, and drawing instruments. Dance classes with barres falling off the wall, and floors with holes. Band Programs lacking the basic instruments they need, orchestras without enough music and music stands for all the members. Most schools do not even offer Industrial Arts programs any longer. Those schools that are lucky enough to have an Industrial Arts program, have equipment that is so outdated, it no longer applies in the real world. The School Arts Fund is a non-profit organization dedicated to changing this. We strive to do this through community involvement with local businesses, government, local artists, teachers, schools, and volunteers.

Goals

  • Identify needs and provide relief to underfunded public school art programs.
  • Recruit artists of all types to donate time, artwork, music, and performances to help raise money.
  • Increase awareness of the lack of funding in the Performing and Visual Arts in public schools.
  • Increase community involvement and awareness of School Arts Fund by hosting with area businesses at local events.
  • Encourage teaching and learning of the industrial arts - basic tool skills that prepare young adults to enter the job marketplace.
  • Identify volunteers, friends of the arts, patrons, and community businesses to get involved in fundraising, and donating to the School Arts Fund.
